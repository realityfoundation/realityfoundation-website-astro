## Overview

RealityFoundation is in its founding phase. We are actively building the team, the research programme, and the network of partners that will define the foundation's first years. There are multiple ways to contribute, depending on who you are and what you bring.

---

## For researchers and doctoral students

We offer researchers the opportunity to publish under the RealityFoundation umbrella, with editorial support, cross-citation within a growing body of work, and visibility with policymakers and institutions who follow our outputs.

If you are working on a thesis or research project related to fake detection, synthetic media, AI governance, or media integrity, we would like to discuss how we can support your work and how it might contribute to our programme.

**Contact us about research opportunities →**

---

## For professors and academic supervisors

We are forming our Scientific Committee and building a network of academic advisors. Involvement offers the opportunity to shape a research agenda at the intersection of computer vision, AI ethics, digital forensics, and public policy — and to contribute to a foundation built for long-term impact.

We can offer positions on the Scientific Committee, co-supervision arrangements for thesis students, and a growing platform for research influence.

**Contact us about academic collaboration →**

---

## For universities and research institutions

We are seeking institutional partnerships with European universities and research centres. If your institution works on topics related to our research agenda, we are open to discussing memoranda of understanding, joint research initiatives, and student exchange arrangements.

**Contact us about institutional partnerships →**

## For journalists and media organisations

Our research is designed to be useful to journalists and newsrooms grappling with the challenge of synthetic media. We are available for comment, background briefings, and longer-form collaborations on investigations related to AI counterfeiting and media integrity.
